# Asana Rebel's Flexible Funnel

Welcome to another episode of _Fix That Funnel_. Today, we're breaking down the **web funnel of Asana Rebel** , one of the biggest apps in the fitness and wellness space. With yoga lessons for both beginners and vets alike, it's a great app if you want to get in shape or just destress from your busy life.

But how flexible is their web2app strategy? To find out, we're enlisting the help of [**Steve P. Young**](linkedin.com/in/stevepyoung), founder of [Appmasters.com](https://appmasters.com/), to explore what‚Äôs working‚Äîand what could use some improvements.

## Web2App Strategy

Asana Rebel supports both App Store and web purchases, here's why that hybrid approach is so powerful for apps in the wellness category:

  * **Higher Willingness to Pay on Web** : Web users are more likely to convert at premium price points and tend to retain longer than app-only subscribers.
  * **Revenue Advantage** : Avoiding Apple‚Äôs 15‚Äì30% cut and only paying a ~2% fee through platforms like Paddle gives brands more revenue to reinvest.
  * **Wider Audience Access** : Web-to-app campaigns unlock new, often under-targeted segments ‚Äî including high-paying Android users.
  * **Ad Flexibility** : Meta, Google, and Link-in-bio campaigns all lead well into web funnels. Sending traffic straight to the app? Less effective, higher drop-off.


## Onboarding

Asana Rebel‚Äôs onboarding starts with a full-screen landing page designed for **mobile-first** engagement ‚Äî not surprising, given most traffic arrives via phones. We're breaking down why this works:

  * **Mobile Behavior Dominates** : Even for web campaigns, users are mostly browsing on mobile. Designing with that in mind is critical.
  * **Ad Channels Shape the Experience** : Meta and Google Ads work better when paired with direct, campaign-specific landers ‚Äî not generic homepages.
  * **Starts with Simple Questions** : Gender is the first question ‚Äî easy, non-invasive, and a quick win for user momentum.

![Gender Question](https://images.prismic.io/paddle-studio/aF66Pnfc4bHWiy6n_FTF-Sxx-Exx-Template-v1.00_05_55_01.Still003.jpg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

  * **Goal-Based Language** : Like other fitness apps, Asana Rebel asks users to define their goals (e.g., lose weight, get toned). Different goals lead to different trial conversion rates ‚Äî a valuable personalization and targeting tool.

![Conversion Rates](https://images.prismic.io/paddle-studio/aF66P3fc4bHWiy6p_FTF-Sxx-Exx-Template-v1.00_06_28_05.Still004.jpg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

  * **Progressive Personalization** : As the flow continues, questions get more personal. This is by design ‚Äî you want to build trust before asking sensitive or reflective questions. There is also the element of price discrimination, where customers can be charged less or more based on their responses within the flow.
  * **Social Proof Appears Late** : Testimonials and trust signals only appear toward the end of the funnel. Ideally, they should be woven throughout.
  * **Loading Screen Psychology** : The final step before the paywall is a ‚Äúcustomizing your experience‚Äù screen with a loading bar ‚Äî a subtle psychological tactic that we've seen boost conversions by ~10‚Äì11% in past tests.

![Loading Bar](https://images.prismic.io/paddle-studio/aF66QXfc4bHWiy6r_FTF-Sxx-Exx-Template-v1.00_07_43_11.Still006.jpg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

Overall, Asana Rebel‚Äôs onboarding is clean, mobile-optimized, and goal-driven ‚Äî a great match for its fitness-first brand positioning.

## Paywall

After onboarding, users hit the paywall. The structure is solid, but there are a few areas for improvement:

  * **Localized Pricing** : The paywall dynamically adjusts currency based on location (USD in the U.S., Euros in Europe, etc.). This increases trust and reduces cognitive friction.
  * **Purchasing Power Sensitivity** : Not all $35.99s are equal. Brands should test regional price adaptations ‚Äî sometimes cutting prices by 50‚Äì90% in lower-income markets ‚Äî and adjust based on actual LTV and CAC.

![Asana Rebel Data](https://images.prismic.io/paddle-studio/aF66Qnfc4bHWiy6s_FTF-Sxx-Exx-Template-v1.00_08_09_06.Still007.jpg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

  * **Payment Options** : Currently, the page supports credit cards and PayPal. While this covers a good chunk of users, options like Apple Pay or Google Pay could reduce checkout friction even further.

![Payment Methods](https://images.prismic.io/paddle-studio/aF66Q3fc4bHWiy6t_FTF-Sxx-Exx-Template-v1.00_08_44_06.Still008.jpg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

  * **Exit Intent Opportunities** : Adding live chat, more testimonials, or a ‚Äúdon‚Äôt go‚Äù offer for hesitating users could rescue conversions.
  * **Missing: Post-Onboarding Personalization** : At this point in the journey, users have already told Asana Rebel their fitness goals ‚Äî but that data isn‚Äôt clearly reflected in the paywall copy or offer framing.


We adding more value-forward language and personalized framing to increase emotional buy-in.

## Download Friction

After the purchase, users are shown a basic app download screen ‚Äî but this moment could be much more powerful.

  * **Use Video** : A welcome video from the founder or coach builds emotional connection. Steve uses this tactic in his own community onboarding.

![Steve Video](https://images.prismic.io/paddle-studio/aF66RXfc4bHWiy6v_FTF-Sxx-Exx-Template-v1.00_09_45_02.Still010.jpg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

  * **QR Code (Especially for Desktop)** : For users who sign up on a computer, make it easy to grab their phone and scan a code.
  * **In-App Plan Hook** : Don‚Äôt just say ‚Äúdownload the app.‚Äù Say ‚Äúyour personalized workout plan is waiting inside‚Äù ‚Äî drive urgency and make the app feel immediately valuable.


The post-purchase experience is one of the most overlooked parts of a web funnel ‚Äî but it‚Äôs also one of the most crucial for app retention.

## üß† Wrap-Up

**What Asana Rebel Does Well:**

  1. **Smooth Onboarding Flow** : The funnel starts with easy questions and keeps users moving without overwhelming them. There‚Äôs no friction, no unnecessary steps ‚Äî just focused progression.
  2. **Solid Pricing and Localization** : The dynamic pricing model adapts to user location, and the design keeps things conversion-focused without being pushy.


**Where It Can Improve:**

  1. **Stronger Use of Social Proof** : Sprinkling testimonials or trust signals earlier and throughout onboarding could ease skepticism before the paywall.
  2. **Deeper Personalization Post-Trial** : Using a user‚Äôs goals to tailor messaging in the paywall and post-signup app flow could create a tighter loop between data input and perceived value.
