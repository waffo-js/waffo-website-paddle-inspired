The short answer is: simultaneous long weekends, and a ‚Äúcorrection‚Äù in sales numbers, after a record-breaking September.

This October, B2B growth rates remained positive, albeit diminished. Examining October‚Äôs growth through its CAGR (compound annual growth rate), tells a similar story - positive, but diminished growth. CAGR **slowed to 3.1%** , from **5.3% in September**. This defied our previous forecast for continued growth, potentially up to 11% this month.

These forecasts were based on two of the driving forces behind SaaS growth - **net new sales** , and **monthly downgrades**. After September‚Äôs sharp recovery from a [seasonal slump](https://www.paddle.com/blog/saas-market-report-july-2024) (as employees took time off for the summer), we noticed that **the rate of new sales still had another ~5% to grow** , in order to revert its pre-slowdown average. Likewise, **the rate of downgrades was still elevated** above its average **by about ~15%**.

![SaaS Index - Oct 2024 - B2B New Sales](https://images.prismic.io/paddle/Z05QvZbqstJ97-D__SaaSIndex-Oct2024-B2BNewSales.jpeg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

  
However, instead of growing by another 5% this October,**the ProfitWell New Sales Index contracted by 3.3%** , reaching an average value of 1.264 over the entire month.

Moreover, while the B2B Downgrade Index dropped as expected, it **only fell by 7.0%, instead of the expected 15%** , reaching an average index value of -2.540.

The Churn Index fell too, by 3.3%, while the rate of upgrades dropped by 8.2%.

![SaaS Index - Oct 2024 - B2B Upgrades](https://images.prismic.io/paddle/Z05QvJbqstJ97-D-_SaaSIndex-Oct2024-B2BUpgrades.jpeg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

##   
Back-to-back holidays stymie B2B growth

So, what caused the slowdown, despite B2B sales having more room to grow? By examining the ProfitWell New Sales index for the month of October, and comparing it to previous years, we find a clue.

For every October since the [Profitwell](https://www.paddle.com/profitwell-metrics) SaaS index was started, we've seen a ~20% dip in new sales that lasts about two weeks, before recovering to previous levels.

![SaaS Index - Oct 2024 - B2B Long Weekend Sales](https://images.prismic.io/paddle/Z05QxJbqstJ97-EG_SaaSIndex-Oct2024-B2BLongWeekendSales.jpeg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

  
This is caused by holidays in the United States (Columbus Day) and Canada (Thanksgiving), falling on the exact same day, and resulting in **simultaneous long weekends for both countries**. Unsurprisingly B2B software sales drop leading up to the holiday, then slowly recover by the end of the month, as employees return to work.

Moreover, while October‚Äôs overall **3.3% drop in new sales** was alleviated by a **7.0% decrease in downgrades** , it‚Äôs important to note that the four drivers of subscription revenue growth (new sales, churn, upgrades and downgrades) do **not** have an equal impact, with **new sales and churn making up the majority of MRR growth** (or contraction) for a typical SaaS company.

![SaaS Index - Oct 2024 - MRR Breakdown](https://images.prismic.io/paddle/Z05QxpbqstJ97-EI_SaaSIndex-Oct2024-MRRBreakdown.png?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

With B2B new sales‚Äô recovery from dual October holidays well under way, it‚Äôs important to note that **we‚Äôre not out of the woods yet**. Typically, November brings **diminished growth rates for B2B** , with companies beginning to plan for the new year, and employees occasionally taking early vacations.

While we expect to see the initial effects of the Fed‚Äôs September interest rate cuts in next month‚Äôs data, it‚Äôs difficult to say whether this will be enough to compensate for the expected sluggish growth, or if the rate cut's effects will only become apparent in the new year.

## B2C growth falters, despite interest rate cuts

![SaaS Index - Oct 2024 - B2C MRR](https://images.prismic.io/paddle/Z05Qv5bqstJ97-EB_SaaSIndex-Oct2024-B2CMRR.jpeg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

October also brought diminished growth to B2C with CAGR **dropping to 3.3%** , after its exceptional rebound in September, where it **averaged 12.7%**.

This brought our B2C MRR Index to a value of 2069 by the end of the month - its highest point since the beginning of this summer‚Äôs unusually long seasonal slowdown.

![SaaS Index - Oct 2024 - B2C CAGR](https://images.prismic.io/paddle/Z05QwJbqstJ97-EC_SaaSIndex-Oct2024-B2CCAGR.jpeg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

Using ProfitWell September data, we [previously predicted](https://www.paddle.com/blog/saas-market-report-september-2024) that B2C would **continue to grow past 12%** , or at least maintain its current CAGR - rather than dropping significantly, as it did this month.

These predictions were based on historical [Profitwell](https://www.paddle.com/profitwell-metrics) growth data, which showed that the B2C market **typically reacts to interest rate changes within one month**.

![SaaS Index - Oct 2024 - B2C CAGR vs Interest Rates](https://images.prismic.io/paddle/Z05QxZbqstJ97-EH_SaaSIndex-Oct2024-B2CCAGRvsInterestRates.jpeg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

Instead, B2C growth faltered, driven down by **across-the-board decreases** in new sales, churn, upgrades and downgrades.

![SaaS Index - Oct 2024 - B2C New Sales](https://images.prismic.io/paddle/Z05QwZbqstJ97-ED_SaaSIndex-Oct2024-B2CNewSales.jpeg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

New sales were hit the hardest, with a **17% drop in October** , as the New Sales Index fell to 0.905; or **just 90.5% the rate of new sales** seen across all ProfitWell B2C companies when the index was started in 2019.

![SaaS Index - Oct 2024 - B2C Churn Downgrades](https://images.prismic.io/paddle/Z05QwpbqstJ97-EE_SaaSIndex-Oct2024-B2CChurnDowngrades.jpeg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

This was followed by downgrades, which **fell by 12.9%** , to an average of -1.456 on the index.

Meanwhile, churn and upgrades showed smaller decreases, of **3.6% and 6.6%** , averaging -1.124 and 1.737 on their respective indices.

![SaaS Index - Oct 2024 - B2C Upgrades](https://images.prismic.io/paddle/Z05Qw5bqstJ97-EF_SaaSIndex-Oct2024-B2CUpgrades.jpeg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

## B2C growth drivers face "correction" & slow for holidays

So what drove the simultaneous decrease in new sales, churn, upgrades and downgrades? Rewinding to September, when B2C growth had rebounded after one of its longest seasonal slowdowns on record, we saw **large spikes across all four growth drivers** :

  * New sales spiked by 41.4%
  * Downgrades spiked by 34%
  * Churn spiked by 7.8%
  * Upgrades spiked by 5.6%


This brought **new sales and downgrades to levels far higher than their 2024 averages**. In particular, new sales were **~20% above their mean** , while **downgrades were elevated by ~10%**.

In the context of these factors **‚Äúreverting to the mean‚Äù** , October‚Äôs 17% drop in new sales, and 12.9% drop in downgrades begins to make more sense.

Moreover, while there was an expectation that the **drop in interest rates** would begin to drive B2C growth in October, rate cuts alone were **unlikely to have overpowered B2C sales' reversion to the mean**.

Finally, it must also be noted that consumer software spend is significantly impacted by the holidays, and historical ProfitWell data shows that B2C growth typically **begins to decline in October** , and continues through to December, before **recovering in the new year**.
