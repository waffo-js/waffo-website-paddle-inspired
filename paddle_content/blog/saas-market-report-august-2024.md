Looking at B2B‚Äôs August performance, you‚Äôd be forgiven for thinking the worst - B2B CAGR dropped down to 0.3%, and an MRR index value of 4169.

![SaaS Index - Aug 2024 - B2B CAGR](https://images.prismic.io/paddle/ZvsI07VsGrYSwK-e_SaaSIndex-Aug2024-B2BCAGR.jpeg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

  
This appears to be a large ‚Äòaftershock‚Äô, or at least a delayed summer slowdown (a seasonal dip in spending, driven by employees taking time off and purchasing less business software), after B2B‚Äôs initial drop to 8.7% CAGR in June, and recovery to 11.5% CAGR in July.

![SaaS Index - Aug 2024 - B2B Slowdown Labelled](https://images.prismic.io/paddle/ZvsI17VsGrYSwK-i_SaaSIndex-Aug2024-B2BSlowdownLabelled.jpeg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

  
We too were lulled into thinking 2024‚Äôs summer slowdown was over by July, based on previous years‚Äô slowdowns recovering by August, at the latest.

By digging into the driving forces of SaaS revenue growth - new sales, churn, upgrades and downgrades, we find indicators that August‚Äôs drop to 0.3% CAGR is indeed a delayed summer slowdown, rather than a new, downwards trend in B2B growth.

![SaaS Index - Aug 2024 - B2B New Sales](https://images.prismic.io/paddle/ZvsI1rVsGrYSwK-h_SaaSIndex-Aug2024-B2BNewSales.jpeg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

Starting with net new sales, we find a 7.1% drop compared to July‚Äôs average, with the Profitwell New Sales Index average at 1.211.

![SaaS Index - Aug 2024 - B2B Upgrades](https://images.prismic.io/paddle/ZvsI2LVsGrYSwK-j_SaaSIndex-Aug2024-B2BUpgrades.jpeg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

  
Upgrades also dropped, falling by 7.0% compared to July‚Äôs average, hitting an index value of 2.428.

![SaaS Index - Aug 2024 - B2B Churn Downgrades](https://images.prismic.io/paddle/ZvsI1LVsGrYSwK-f_SaaSIndex-Aug2024-B2BChurnDowngrades.jpeg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

Churn dropped too, decreasing to an index value of 1.543, a 6.5% drop compared to July - putting some upwards pressure on B2B growth, but not enough to counteract the drop in new sales and upgrades.

Downgrades, on the other hand, remained relatively stable, dropping by only 0.5% compared to July‚Äôs average, reaching an index value of -2.369.

## A classic seasonal slowdown

By taking all of these growth factors into account, we find the ‚Äúfingerprint‚Äù of a (delayed) seasonal slowdown - a drop in net new sales and upgrades - as employees take time off of work for the summer, and spend less time purchasing and using business software.

As such, we still stand by our previous assessment that we‚Äôll see a quick recovery to our [projected 11% CAGR for B2B SaaS](https://www.paddle.com/blog/saas-market-report-may-2024), albeit in September, instead of August.

In fact, by including the Fed‚Äôs rate 0.5% interest rate cut in our projections, we expect **even stronger** growth in the last quarter of 2024.

![SaaS Index - Aug 2024 - B2B CAGR vs Interest Rates](https://images.prismic.io/paddle/ZvsI0rVsGrYSwK-d_SaaSIndex-Aug2024-B2BCAGRvsInterestRates.jpeg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

By examining Profitwell Metrics data on previous rate changes in 2020 and 2022, we find a consistent 2 month lag between changes in interest rates, and their effect on the B2B SaaS market.

Accordingly, we can confidently forecast that B2B SaaS will recover to 11% CAGR in September, and remain at that level until November, where **B2B will begin to grow past 11% CAGR**.

We still expect a temporary dip in December due to the winter holidays - however, B2B will quickly recover in January of 2025 (according to previous years‚Äô data) - then continue to **accelerate beyond 11% CAGR for the remainder of the year** , should the Fed continues to cut interest rates, as they have been forecasted to.

## B2C recovery, despite August's negative CAGR

![SaaS Index - Aug 2024 - B2C MRR](https://images.prismic.io/paddle/ZvsI3bVsGrYSwK-o_SaaSIndex-Aug2024-B2CMRR.jpeg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

At the surface level, B2C‚Äôs growth also appears to be dismal, with August's CAGR averaging to -5.6%, and an MRR index value of 2044 (down from 2053 in July).

![SaaS Index - Aug 2024 - B2C CAGR](https://images.prismic.io/paddle/ZvsI2rVsGrYSwK-l_SaaSIndex-Aug2024-B2CCAGR.jpeg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

This would make 2024‚Äôs summer slowdown the longest for B2C that we‚Äôve seen since the summer of 2022 (when the market was at its most volatile in several years).

![SaaS Index - Aug 2024 - B2C Slowdown Labelled](https://images.prismic.io/paddle/ZvsI37VsGrYSwK-q_SaaSIndex-Aug2024-B2CSlowdownLabelled.jpeg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

However, August's CAGR of -5.6% was a significant jump from July‚Äôs -9.5% CAGR, and by examining CAGR data at the **day level** , instead of as a **monthly average** , we find clear evidence that **B2C has started to recover from its summer slowdown**.

## A full recovery, in just a week?

![SaaS Index - Aug 2024 - B2C Daily CAGR](https://images.prismic.io/paddle/ZvsI3LVsGrYSwK-n_SaaSIndex-Aug2024-B2CDailyCAGR.jpeg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

Here, we find that B2C CAGR grew steadily into the positives in the latter half of August, compensating for a dismal start to the month, **even averaging to 6.7% CAGR in the final week of the month**.

Again, looking at the driving forces behind B2C growth - new sales, churn, upgrades and downgrades - we find that our August recovery was driven by **all four** growth factors, with an increase in new sales and upgrades, and a drop in churn and downgrades (with the latter falling significantly).

![SaaS Index - Aug 2024 - B2C New Sales](https://images.prismic.io/paddle/ZvsI3rVsGrYSwK-p_SaaSIndex-Aug2024-B2CNewSales.jpeg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

Examining the new sales index, we find that new sales grew **significantly** in the last half of August, after a sluggish start to the month.

The sales index reached a peak value of 0.856 on August 31, despite averaging to just 0.771 over the entire month. As such, August's overall average was only 0.9% higher than July's, despite a rapid recovery in the final week.

![SaaS Index - Aug 2024 - B2C Churn Downgrades](https://images.prismic.io/paddle/ZvsI27VsGrYSwK-m_SaaSIndex-Aug2024-B2CChurnDowngrades.jpeg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

Downgrades however, were the main driver of B2C‚Äôs August recovery, dropping by an **enormous** **27.6%** , to an average of -1.248 on the Profitwell downgrade index.

A 3.2% drop in churn also contributed (albeit very little) to B2C‚Äôs recovery, with the churn index falling to an average of -1.082 in August.

![SaaS Index - Aug 2024 - B2C Upgrades](https://images.prismic.io/paddle/ZvsI4LVsGrYSwK-r_SaaSIndex-Aug2024-B2CUpgrades.jpeg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

Finally, upgrades increased by 6.2% compared to July, reaching an index value of 1.762, after a large spike in upgrades towards the end of August.

## B2C positioned for a strong 2025

Based on B2C‚Äôs rapid recovery in the last week of August, it‚Äôs clear that B2C growth will fully recover to its [previously forecasted levels of 8% CAGR](https://www.paddle.com/blog/saas-market-report-may-2024) by the time September‚Äôs growth data is available.

In fact, by including the Fed‚Äôs rate cut to 4.83% in our predictions, we expect B2C to follow a similar trajectory to B2B in the last half of the year.

![SaaS Index - Aug 2024 - B2C CAGR vs Interest Rates](https://images.prismic.io/paddle/ZvsLr7VsGrYSwK_f_SaaSIndex-Aug2024-B2CCAGRvsInterestRates.jpeg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

Profitwell Metrics data from 2020 and 2022 shows that the B2C SaaS market responds to changes in interest rates with a 1 month lag.

As such, we expect B2C growth to rebound to 8% CAGR in September, then gradually accelerate beyond 8% CAGR from October to December, where it will temporarily drop due to the winter holidays.

From there, we expect B2C growth to fully recover in January, and continue to slowly accelerate for the rest of 2025, should the Fed continue to drop interest rates.

## Strategies to consider

Barring seasonal slowdowns and black swan events, the B2B and B2C SaaS markets are poised for steady growth, especially with the Fed forecasted to drop interest rates by another 2%, throughout 2025.

## Improve retention by building trust

Building customer trust is key with subscription software products, because your customers pay you continuously, and expect continuous value. You can reinforce the value your product offers by communicating your product vision and actively seeking customer feedback. This will help create a sense of ownership in your product, and create the impression that your product is **becoming more valuable over time**.

Consider using changelogs and in-app messaging to keep your users informed about updates and improvements. We also recommend building customer feedback panels and investing in content & events to share the latest improvements to your products.

## Grow trust with transparent subscriptions

Transparency around subscriptions, cancellations, and payment flows is another essential retention strategy. Customers who feel in control of their subscription are more likely to stay loyal, while expansion through upsells and cross-sells becomes smoother.

If it‚Äôs easy for customers to update or modify their subscriptions, they will be less anxious about upgrading their subscriptions or purchasing additional products, boosting your conversion rate.
