## Clear skies ahead for B2B

While a growth rate of 5.3% CAGR marks a solid recovery from the chaos of summer, it‚Äôs clear that **B2B has even more room to grow**.

Examining Profitwell‚Äôs growth data _at the day-level,_ we find that **growth has steadily trended upwards** throughout the month, with the third week of September **averaging a growth rate of nearly 13% CAGR**.

![SaaS Index - Sept 2024 - B2B CAGR Daily](https://images.prismic.io/paddle/ZyRfWa8jQArT0Gxo_SaaSIndex-Sept2024-B2BCAGRDaily.jpeg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

  
Analyzing the Profitwell B2B Indices (tracking new sales, churn, upgrades and downgrades) we also find signs pointing to B2B‚Äôs continued growth.

Here we see a **burst of activity,** with new sales, churn, upgrades and downgrades **all increasing in September**. This is consistent with a ‚Äúrebound‚Äù from a summer slump, as the employees who took time off, return to the office and work through a backlog of software purchasing decisions - integrating new vendors, buying more seats, or even switching software providers.

![SaaS Index - Sept 2024 - B2B New Sales](https://images.prismic.io/paddle/ZyRfWq8jQArT0Gxp_SaaSIndex-Sept2024-B2BNewSales.jpeg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

Starting with new sales, we find a 7.9% increase compared to August, with the sales index averaging to 1.307 over September.

![SaaS Index - Sept 2024 - B2B Churn Downgrades](https://images.prismic.io/paddle/ZyRfW68jQArT0Gxq_SaaSIndex-Sept2024-B2BChurnDowngrades.jpeg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

Churn grew by 6.3%, hitting an average index value of -1.641, while downgrades increased by 15.3%, averaging -2.731 on the index.

![SaaS Index - Sept 2024 - B2B Upgrades](https://images.prismic.io/paddle/ZyRfXK8jQArT0Gxr_SaaSIndex-Sept2024-B2BUpgrades.jpeg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

Finally, upgrades grew by 11.2%, reaching an average index value of 2.699.

With summer over, and employees settling back into their routines, we can expect this ‚Äúburst of activity‚Äù to subside, and the new sales, churn, upgrade, and downgrade indices to settle back to their nominal values, from before the slowdown.

Taking this into account, we predict that the new sales index will climb another ~5%, back to its pre-summer average; while the downgrade index falls ~15% to settle on its average value from before the slowdown.

![SaaS Index - Sept 2024 - B2B CAGR vs Interest Rates](https://images.prismic.io/paddle/ZyRfVK8jQArT0Gxj_SaaSIndex-Sept2024-B2BCAGRvsInterestRates.jpeg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

## A surge in sales (and everything else)

The driving forces of B2C growth - new sales, churn, upgrades and downgrades - point to a recovery similar to the B2B market. Consumers returned from the summer holidays this September, starting work and school again, and generally spending more time indoors.

This drove a ‚Äúburst of activity‚Äù across our new sales, churn, upgrade and downgrade indices, as consumers‚Äô general usage of subscription software increased, and they made decisions about whether to upgrade, downgrade, or switch subscription software products.

![SaaS Index - Sept 2024 - B2C New Sales](https://images.prismic.io/paddle/ZyRfYK8jQArT0Gxv_SaaSIndex-Sept2024-B2CNewSales.jpeg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

The Profitwell new sales index saw the greatest increase, making up for 3 months of declining sales, and hitting an average index value of 1.089. Driven by an enormous 41.4% surge, this was the largest spike seen since April 2020, during the volatile opening days of the pandemic.

![SaaS Index - Sept 2024 - B2C Churn Downgrades](https://images.prismic.io/paddle/ZyRfYa8jQArT0Gxw_SaaSIndex-Sept2024-B2CChurnDowngrades.jpeg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

The churn index grew by 7.8% in September, to an average value of -1.167, its highest since Feb 2023. Downgrades on the other hand, grew significantly, reaching an average index value of -1.672, after growing by a whopping 34% in September.

![SaaS Index - Sept 2024 - B2C Upgrades](https://images.prismic.io/paddle/ZyRfYq8jQArT0Gxy_SaaSIndex-Sept2024-B2CUpgrades.jpeg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

The increase in upgrades however, was comparatively minor, growing only 5.6% in September, and reaching an average index value of 1.860.

Similar to what we observed in B2B, after this burst of activity across all four B2C growth drivers subsides, we can expect these indices to return to their nominal values (from before the summer slowdown).

This means the new sales index will likely fall by ~12%, churn will drop by ~9%, while upgrades and downgrades fall by ~15% and ~11% respectively. This will likely have a **cooling effect** on B2C growth, however, this will be working in opposition to the **upwards pressure on B2C growth** exerted by the Fed‚Äôs September rate cut.

Accordingly, we are likely to see B2C growth fluctuate around 12% CAGR over the next few months (potentially even dropping by a few percentage points), before slowing down for the holidays again in December.

## Trust is the foundation for retention

Building and maintaining customer trust is one of the most important steps to creating an exceptionally sticky product.

If you run a SaaS company, the bar for **proving your value to your customers** is much higher than products which are one-off purchases. Your customers pay for your product **monthly** , and they will ask themselves whether your product is worth it, each time they see your company on their credit card bill.

This means that ensuring your product continuously solves your customers‚Äô core problems is merely table stakes. To maintain a high retention rate, you need to proactively share your product roadmap (and make sure it‚Äôs aligned with your customers‚Äô vision for the future), call attention to new features, and maintain channels for customer feedback.

Investing in in-product communication channels, and regular launch events are an exceptional way to stay top of mind with your customers, and show them that they‚Äôre subscribing to a product that gets better every day. We also suggest ‚Äústarting small‚Äù when seeking customer feedback, and only asking for in-person customer interviews once a rapport has been established.

## Unlock revenue with value based pricing

Often, SaaS companies will use seats as a proxy for measuring product usage, thinking that as their customers scale, the number of seats they will pay for, will grow too.

However, this creates misaligned incentives - customers often try to reduce the number of seats they buy, by sharing passwords or scaling back their usage - potentially reducing your product‚Äôs overall stickiness.

So, instead of using seats as a pricing metric, try pricing your product based on total usage. This way, pricing will be more transparent, and far more of your customers‚Äô employees will access (and share) your product.

Moreover, studies have shown that willingness to pay **does not usually scale with your customers‚Äô company size** , but it **does scale with their usage**. So, by switching from seat-based pricing to value-based pricing, you will likely be able to significantly increase your revenue from top-line customers.
