This July, B2B SaaS growth made a full recovery to pre-summer levels, after experiencing diminished growth in June.

July‚Äôs growth reached 11.5% CAGR - closely in line with our predictions for an average of [11% CAGR in 2024](https://www.paddle.com/blog/saas-market-report-may-2024).

![Saas Index - July 2024 - B2B Summer Slowdown](https://images.prismic.io/paddle/Zs-kYUaF0TcGJfjF_SaaSIndex-July2024-B2BCAGRSummerAnnotated.jpeg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

In fact, this is the softest summer slowdown for B2B since the SaaS Index was created in 2019; with a dip in CAGR of only 19%. This can likely be attributed to B2B‚Äôs unusually high stability this year, as interest rates have been held steady for the last 12 months.

## Sales rebound, downgrades settle

Digging deeper, into the [Profitwell](https://www.paddle.com/profitwell-metrics) indices for factors that **directly** drive growth - net new sales, churn, upgrades & downgrades - we find that July‚Äôs recovery was driven by a rebound in new sales, while downgrades settled back to normal levels.

![SaaS Index - July 2024 - B2B New Sales](https://images.prismic.io/paddle/Zs-kZEaF0TcGJfjJ_SaaSIndex-July2024-B2BNewSales.jpeg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

_For the ProfitWell B2B SaaS Sales Index, a 1.00 reading represents sales on an ‚Äúaverage‚Äù day in 2019, while a 1.10 reading would be 10% higher sales. The ProfitWell B2B SaaS Churn Index is calculated similarly, but will be negative, with -1.00 being an ‚Äúaverage‚Äù 2019 figure)._

Examining the New Sales index, we find a 6% increase in the index‚Äôs average value, to 1.303, compared to June‚Äôs average of 1.230.

While this does represent a rebound from June‚Äôs slow sales, it‚Äôs important to note that New Sales would need to grow another 4.8% to reach its 2024 average, before the summer slowdown. This is a potential indicator that B2B has _further_ to recover, beyond the 11.5% CAGR reached this month.

![Saas Index - July 2024 - B2B Churn and Downgrades](https://images.prismic.io/paddle/Zs-kYkaF0TcGJfjH_SaaSIndex-July2024-B2BChurnDowngrades.jpeg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

Downgrades also drove B2B‚Äôs July recovery, with a 7.7% drop to an index value of -2.381, down from its 2024 high in June, of -2.580.

It‚Äôs also worth noting that downgrades need to drop by another 5% before fully recovering to levels before the summer slowdown - leaving open the door to growth beyond 11.5% CAGR for B2B in the coming months.

On the other hand, both churn and upgrades have remained stable throughout the summer, and beforehand - July included. They have neither driven the summer slowdown, nor the subsequent recovery.

Churn decreased by 1.0% in July, to an average index value of -1.651 (ie, 1.651x the rate of churn seen when the index was started in 2019).

![Saas Index - July 2024 - B2B Upgrades](https://images.prismic.io/paddle/Zs-kZUaF0TcGJfjK_SaaSIndex-July2024-B2BUpgrades.jpeg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

  
Upgrades were even more stable in July, with a near-zero decrease (-0.1%) from June, dropping to an index value of 2.610.

## B2C remains sluggish for the summer

B2B slowed to a dismal -9.5% CAGR in July, continuing its fall after dropping to a negative growth rate in June. This is unsurprising, especially compared to B2B‚Äôs quick recovery, as business software spend is far more insulated from individual employees taking time off for the summer.

![Saas Index - July 2024 - B2C MRR](https://images.prismic.io/paddle/Zs-kaUaF0TcGJfjP_SaaSIndex-July2024-B2CMRR.jpeg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

The B2C SaaS Index dropped to a value of 2052 - its lowest since February, after months of consistent growth since the beginning of 2024.

![Saas Index - July 2024 - B2C CAGR](https://images.prismic.io/paddle/Zs-kZ0aF0TcGJfjM_SaaSIndex-July2024-B2CCAGR.jpeg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

Barring a 1-day spike at the beginning of the month, all of July saw negative CAGR on a day-to-day basis.

Looking back at historical B2C CAGR data however, shows that B2C‚Äôs June / July performance, while scary, is not abnormal, and will likely revert to pre-summer levels in August.

![Saas Index - July 2024 - B2C Summer Slowdown](https://images.prismic.io/paddle/Zs-kZkaF0TcGJfjL_SaaSIndex-July2024-B2CCAGRSummerAnnotated.jpeg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

In fact, B2C‚Äôs 2024 summer dip of 375% between May and July is fairly typical, with 2022‚Äôs dip far exceeding it at 500% (granted, 2024 was an extremely volatile year for SaaS). Moreover, data from the past 5 years of the Profitwell SaaS index shows that recovery consistently happens by August of the same year.

## B2C sales still sluggish, downgrades elevated

Examining the factors driving growth - new sales, churn, upgrades & downgrades - we find that the majority of B2C‚Äôs July slowdown came from a slowdown in sales and a jump in downgrades.  


![Saas Index - July 2024 - B2C New Sales](https://images.prismic.io/paddle/Zs-kakaF0TcGJfjS_SaaSIndex-July2024-B2CNewSales.jpeg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

The New Sales index dropped significantly, by 9.8%, to an average value of 0.763 in July - its lowest value in 18 months.

![Saas Index - July 2024 - B2C Churn and Downgrades](https://images.prismic.io/paddle/Zs-kaEaF0TcGJfjN_SaaSIndex-July2024-B2CChurnDowngrades.jpeg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

Meanwhile, churn has remained stable, with a 1.1% decrease to an index value of -1.118.

Downgrades on the other hand, increased by a whopping 16.6%, to an average value of -1.723 - the highest downgrade index value seen since March 2020.

![Saas Index - July 2024 - B2C Upgrades](https://images.prismic.io/paddle/Zs-ka0aF0TcGJfjU_SaaSIndex-July2024-B2CUpgrades.jpeg?auto=format%2Ccompress&fit=max&w=3840)

Finally, the Upgrade Index saw a moderate increase of 7.1%, to a value of 1.659 - continuing a slight upwards trend seen in June, where upgrades saw an increase of 5.2%.

While the upwards movement in upgrades **was not enough** to offset B2C‚Äôs overall summer slowdown, it is worth keeping an eye on, in the coming months. After B2C‚Äôs likely recovery in August, continually increasing upgrades may push B2C‚Äôs overall growth rate above our prediction of 8% CAGR for 2024.

**Strategies to consider**

## Anticipating seasonality

As we‚Äôve seen, B2C (and to a lesser extent, B2B) software spend is prone to large, regular swings in growth, sales, and upgrades throughout the year. Knowing **when** these periods of decreased spending are likely to occur, can help you manage your cash flow - even providing you the opportunity to mitigate slowdowns and keep growth stable.

By examining revenue data from Profitwell Metrics, we‚Äôve identified a few of these annual slowdowns. On average, we find their effect on revenue growth to be as follows:

  * June & July (B2C): -290%
  * June & July (B2B): -32%
  * November & December (B2C): -164%
  * November & December (B2B): -121%

## Be careful with discounts

You might be tempted to offer seasonal discounts to boost revenue during slowdowns, or encourage sales during holiday surges like Black Friday.

However, you must be extremely thoughtful when building your discount strategy, as you run the risk of **devaluing your product offering.** By discounting your SaaS, you lower the perceived value of your product to customers, and lower their willingness to pay over the long-term - **increasing future churn** , as the discount expires or your customer switches to another discounted competitor.

Instead of using discounting to drive growth during slowdowns, consider adding a ‚Äúfree‚Äù or cheaper ‚Äúentry-tier‚Äù to your offering, allowing more price sensitive customers to experience the value of your product before being prompted to upgrade, to access more features.

Another option is offering extra value (e.g., more seats or additional services) at your current pricing, for a limited time. Like discounts, this tactic can drive urgency and give customers the feeling that they scored a great deal - without hurting your product‚Äôs perceived value in your customers‚Äô eyes.

## Find new efficiencies to maintain growth

While navigating a period of slow growth, **segmenting your customers** , and applying different pricing strategies to each group is an excellent way to expand your customer base - including those with less willingness to pay, while also serving your customers with the greatest need (at premium prices).
